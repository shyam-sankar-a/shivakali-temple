Primary Deity

Goddess Kali

Mahakali Bhadrakali Kalaratri Chamunda

Goddess Kali is the principal deity of this temple. She is the fearsome yet compassionate Mother who stands on the chest of Lord Shiva, symbolising the conquest of the ego and attachment. Her dark complexion represents the infinite cosmos, her garland of skulls the cycle of birth and death, and her four arms — holding a sword, a severed head, and offering the gestures of protection and boon — embody the totality of time and power.

Devotees come to her seeking courage in adversity, liberation from fear, and protection from evil. The goddess is especially venerated during Navratri, Kali Puja, and the annual Ulsavam festival.

Significance at this Temple

The Shivakali form worshipped here is a synthesis of Shaiva and Shakta traditions — the combined energy of Shiva and Kali. This unique form grants both worldly blessings and spiritual liberation to sincere devotees.

Warrior Mother

Goddess Durga

Bhagavathi Amba Chandika Mahishasuramardini

Goddess Durga, also honoured as Amba Bhagavathi in this temple, is the invincible warrior who vanquished the buffalo-demon Mahishasura after eighteen days of battle. She is the collective power of all devas, riding a lion and armed with weapons gifted by each god, representing the triumph of good over evil.

The name "Amba" means Mother — a reminder that behind the fierce warrior is the nurturing, all-loving mother who protects her children with absolute devotion. She is worshipped for strength, protection, prosperity, and removal of obstacles.

Significance at this Temple

The Amba Bhagavathi form here is worshipped in the Kerala tradition with Thidambu Nritham and Theyyam-inspired rituals during the annual festival, connecting modern devotees to the ancient temple arts of Kerala.
